A hydraulic car lift is a mechanical device that uses pressurized fluid to raise and lower vehicles, operating on Pascal's principle. It works by pumping oil into a cylinder, which pushes a piston up to lift the vehicle; to lower it, a valve opens, allowing the fluid to flow back to the reservoir and the vehicle's weight to push the piston down. Common types include two-post lifts for general maintenance, four-post lifts for stability and storage, scissor lifts for space efficiency in detailing or tire changes, and mobile column lifts for large vehicles like buses or trucks.
